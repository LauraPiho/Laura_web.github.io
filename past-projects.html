Past Projects

CostAdapt

2022-2024

Adapting to the rapidly changing Arctic

Implications of changing hydrodynamic conditions on the coastal-marine environments in Svalbard: A joint study of Tallinn University of Technology and The University Center in Svalbard.

Key Achievements:
  • Analysis of Arctic coastal changes
  • Hydrodynamic modeling of Svalbard waters
  • Climate adaptation strategies
Collaboration:

Joint research with The University Center in Svalbard (UNIS)

MAMMAMIA

2020-2023

Multi-scAle-Multi-Method Analysis of Mechanisms causing Ice Acceleration

Comprehensive study investigating the complex mechanisms behind ice acceleration in polar regions using multi-scale analysis approaches.

Research Focus:
  • Ice dynamics analysis
  • Multi-scale modeling approaches
  • Acceleration mechanism identification
Impact:

Enhanced understanding of polar ice behavior and climate implications

ROBIMINERS

2019-2023

Resilient Bio-inspired Modular Robotic Miners

Development of bio-inspired robotic systems for mining applications, focusing on modular design and resilient operation in challenging environments.

Innovation Areas:
  • Bio-inspired robotic design
  • Modular system architecture
  • Resilient operation protocols
Applications:

Mining automation and extreme environment robotics

Project aiming to develop new sensors for flow sensing in coastal and river environments, advancing our understanding of natural water systems.

Advanced sensing capabilities for environmental monitoring

Research Legacy
